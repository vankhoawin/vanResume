About Me

 

I am majoring in Computer Science at the University of California, Irvine. The passion to create has always been an integral part of my life -- from playing with Lego, to making electronic music on my computer, and now also, programming. As a computer scientist, we are given tools from which we can build anything imaginable; as they say, the world is your oyster. In particular, I am interested in networked systems. It is amazing how different devices can interact from any part of the world in a matter of seconds. Machine code is truly, the universal language.

 

Learning computer science in my studies has not been enough. As well as programming independently, I have been selected as a research assistant by Professor Gillian Hayes to be a part of the Social and Technological Action Research (STAR) group and Laboratory for Ubiquitous Computing and Interaction (LUCI). For our project, Visual Schedule (Vsked for short), I work on back-end coding alongside other members.

 

As said before, I love producing and listening to music: Radiohead, Fleet Foxes, The Tallest Man on Earth, Burial and Squarepusher are some of my favorite artists and largest inspirations. Photography and hiking are also two interests of mine. In many ways, programming is alike my interests; for a good end product they require experience, knowledge, and a bit of creativity. Some computer scientists see programming as a means to an end; they certainly are high in demand, are in a lucrative field, etc. However, for me I'd like to say that programming is an end in itself: there is something just so rewarding about a finished program, that, like a photographer done processing his photos in the dark room, he can't wait another second to start on the next project.

 

Future Goals

 

When I am experienced enough, I would love to build a visual synthesizer as an Android app. This would be a major step in terms of programming proficiency and is an intersection of code, sound and visuals.
